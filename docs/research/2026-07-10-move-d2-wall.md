# Moving the D2 Wall: Verification-Limited Dysarthric Voice-Command Recognition

## TL;DR
- The binding metric (D2: FRR@FAR≤5% for in-vocabulary confusor rejection) is fixed at ~55% because within-word intra-speaker variance ≳ between-command distance; no admissible lever moves this in the severe tail, so the highest-value bets are **P3** (variance-aware multi-vector/DTW trajectory scoring — the only approach with direct dysarthric validation) and **P1** (score-domain calibration/normalization), which attack the variance structure rather than chasing a better encoder.
- **P2** (trainable backend on frozen SSL embeddings) is theoretically well-matched to a within-variance-dominated problem but is currently an **external-validity risk**: there is no published, isolated PLDA/neural-PLDA/adapter-vs-cosine measurement on dysarthric embeddings; the strongest effect sizes are typical-speech or child-speech only.
- For the very-severe tail (D2≈90%, AUC≈0.70 near information-theoretically capped), no admissible voice-only verifier delivers a deployable operating point; the only usable product is a **P5** system reframe — per-severity operating points plus an abstain+confirm turn and/or a complementary modality (switch, dwell, gaze).

## Key Findings
1. **The wall is real and encoder-invariant.** SSL front-ends lift closed-set dysarthric accuracy walls but not verification separability. This is consistent with the published dysarthric verification literature, in which every measured gain comes from front-end feature fusion + augmentation, never from a separability-raising backend on frozen embeddings.
2. **P1 is the most direct untried attack on a fixed-AUC-at-fixed-FAR wall, but its evidence is off-target.** Score normalization (AS-norm, S-norm) and non-monotonic quality-aware calibration can move FRR at a fixed FAR, but every quantified gain is on typical speech (VoxCeleb), and the mechanism (cohort/nuisance calibration) does not obviously address CV≈1 genuine-score dispersion. Expected AUC gain at CV≈1 is modest — a few points, not 0.70→0.85 alone.
3. **P3 has the strongest dysarthria-specific evidence.** Latent Phrase Matching (Apple, Interspeech 2023) — a DTW-over-latent-embeddings query-by-example approach — showed **a 60% recall improvement over a commercial ASR system on an internal dataset of 32 people with dysarthria, and a 30.5% accuracy improvement on the public Italian EasyCall corpus**, and the authors report "this approach works regardless of severity." It directly addresses the within-word trajectory variability that a single utterance vector destroys.
4. **P4 (enrollment-side) is low-risk, low-ceiling.** Multi-exemplar enrollment and quality selection reliably help, but move the operating point modestly and do not change the underlying variance decomposition.
5. **P5 is the only route to a usable product in the severe tail:** per-severity operating points, abstain+confirm turns, and complementary modalities.

## Details

### P1 — SCORE-DOMAIN (highest theoretical priority)
**Candidate methods:**
- **Adaptive S-norm (AS-norm)** — Matejka et al., "Analysis of Score Normalization in Multilingual Speaker Recognition," Interspeech 2017; Karam et al. 2011. Cohort-based per-trial mean/variance normalization using top-scoring impostor cohort.
- **Trainable AS-norm (TAS-norm)** — Choi et al., "Trainable Adaptive Score Normalization for Automatic Speaker Verification," 2025 (arXiv:2504.04512). Learnable impostor embeddings; **4.11% relative EER and 10.62% relative minDCF improvement over AS-norm** on VoxCeleb1-O with ECAPA-TDNN.
- **Quality-aware score calibration (QMF)** — Thienpondt, Desplanques, Demuynck, "The IDLAB VoxSRC-20 Submission," 2020 (arXiv:2010.11255). Non-monotonic logistic calibration with quality features. Because the calibration map is non-monotonic, it can improve fixed-threshold metrics such as EER and minDCF; large-margin fine-tuning contributed ~15% relative EER, with further QMF gains.
- **Phonetic-richness / quality calibration** — Pindrop, "Phonetic Richness for Improved Automatic Speaker Verification," 2024 (arXiv:2407.08017). **5.8% relative EER improvement** on VoxCeleb1; benefit grows for short utterances with repeated words — the closest structural analogue to a fixed command vocabulary.
- **Conformal prediction / reject-option calibration** — Johansson, Löfström et al., MDAI 2023; Linusson et al., PAKDD 2018. Distribution-free reject option with guaranteed singleton error rate; reported to outperform Platt scaling. Natural fit for the P5 abstain outcome.
- **Quantization-based score calibration for DTW-KWS** — Wilkinghoff & Cornaggia-Urrigshardt, 2025 (arXiv:2510.15432). Embedding quantization + quantization-error normalization before DTW thresholding; significantly improves detection-threshold robustness in noisy few-shot KWS.

**Effect sizes:** all on typical speech; relative EER improvements in the 4–15% range. **None measured on dysarthric confusor rejection. EXTERNAL-VALIDITY RISK: HIGH.**

**On-device feasibility:** Excellent. AS-norm/S-norm/QMF are arithmetic over enrollment-time cohort statistics — near-zero compute, fully deterministic, no cloud. Conformal calibration is a quantile lookup. All fit trivially in the ≤150 MB / INT8 / NNAPI envelope.

**Assessment — PASS on admissibility, UNCERTAIN on the wall.** Score normalization corrects cross-trial/cohort miscalibration, not the CV≈1 genuine-score dispersion that is the measured root cause. Per-command adaptive thresholds and non-monotonic quality calibration are the sub-levers most likely to help, because they can locally reshape the genuine/impostor overlap per command; but expected standalone AUC gain at CV≈1 is small.

### P2 — REPRESENTATION-DOMAIN (trainable backend on frozen SSL)
**Candidate methods:**
- **Neural PLDA (NPLDA)** — Ramoji, Krishnan, Ganapathy, "NPLDA: A Deep Neural PLDA Model for Speaker Verification," 2020 (arXiv:2002.03562); end-to-end variant (arXiv:2008.04527). Discriminative likelihood-ratio scoring optimized on a DCF surrogate.
- **Discriminative/constrained PLDA** — Wang, Lee, Liu, "Scoring of Large-Margin Embeddings for Speaker Verification: Cosine or PLDA?", Interspeech 2022 (arXiv:2204.03965). Constrained/diagonal within-covariance PLDA "outperforms cosine scoring consistently with reductions in EER and minDCF by 10.9% and 4.9%, respectively," and by 40.8% EER / 35.1% minDCF over conventional PLDA — where within-speaker covariance is large.
- **DCA-PLDA robust backend** — Ferrer, McLaren, Brümmer, "A Speaker Verification Backend with Robust Performance across Conditions," Computer Speech & Language 2022 (arXiv:2102.01760). Joint discriminative PLDA + adaptive calibrator.
- **Prototypical / metric-learning few-shot heads** — Snell et al. 2017; Wang, Wang, Law, Rudzicz, Brudno, "Centroid-based deep metric learning for speaker recognition," ICASSP 2019.

**Effect sizes (subagent-verified):** Direct PLDA-vs-cosine comparisons exist **only on typical speech (VoxCeleb/SITW)**: discriminative PLDA gives 15–21% relative EER over generative PLDA (Borgström, MIT-LL, SITW); on modern large-margin embeddings **cosine beats PLDA by 51.61% relative EER (and 50.73% relative DCF₀.₀₁) on VoxCeleb**, per Peng, He, Ding, Lee & Wan, "Unifying Cosine and PLDA Back-ends for Speaker Verification," 2022 (arXiv:2204.10523) — i.e., a trainable backend only helps when the embedding's within-speaker covariance has not already been shrunk by large-margin training. The high-within-variance analogue is child speech: Afshan, Guo, Park, Ravi, McCree, Alwan (Interspeech 2020, arXiv:2008.03616) show PLDA adaptation reduces style-mismatch EER from 22.12%→18.75% and 3.03%→2.69%. **Dysarthric-specific: NO isolated backend-vs-cosine measurement exists.** Salim, Shahnawazuddin & Ahmad's dysarthric work (Applied Acoustics 2023, 109412; Speech Communication 2024) reports front-end feature-fusion+augmentation gains only: MFCC+LFCC fusion gives 25.78% relative EER reduction (i-vector) and 23.66% (x-vector); MFCC+CQCC gives 15.07%/22.75% (Circuits, Systems & Signal Processing 2023). Closed-set dysarthric speaker identification reached **87.74% (text-dependent mode, UASpeech)**, per Farhadipour & Veisi, "Gammatonegram Representation for End-to-End Dysarthric Speech Processing Tasks," Iran J. Computer Science 2024 (arXiv:2307.03296) — a gammatonegram-CNN, not WavLM.

**On-device feasibility:** PLDA/LDA/WCCN are small linear-algebra models (a few MB), deterministic, INT8-friendly. An NPLDA or adapter head trained offline on out-of-domain dysarthric corpora and frozen at deploy is admissible. PASS on envelope.

**Assessment — HIGH-POTENTIAL but EXTERNAL-VALIDITY RISK.** The theoretical case is strong precisely because within-speaker covariance dominates — the regime PLDA/LDA/WCCN were designed for, and the regime where the child-speech analogue shows the largest backend gains. But applying these numbers to dysarthria is currently extrapolation. It must be measured on held-out, per-severity dysarthric data, with the leakage controls that sank the refuted nuisance-subspace and female-only whitening results.

### P3 — VARIABILITY MODELING (multi-vector / trajectory / DTW)
**Candidate methods:**
- **Latent Phrase Matching (LPM)** — Lea, Yee, Narain, Huang, Tooley, Bigham, Findlater (Apple), "Latent Phrase Matching for Dysarthric Speech," Interspeech 2023 (arXiv:2306.05446). QBE with DTW over latent keyword-spotter embeddings (frame rate 100 Hz), per-phrase within-phrase threshold τᵢ set from enrollment-time DTW distances. **60% recall improvement over commercial ASR on 32 dysarthric speakers; 30.5% accuracy improvement on EasyCall; works regardless of severity. STRONGEST dysarthria-validated candidate and best admissibility fit** (on-device, language/vocabulary-agnostic, no training at onboarding).
- **Multi-sample DTW** — Wilkinghoff & Cornaggia-Urrigshardt, "Multi-Sample Dynamic Time Warping for Few-Shot Keyword Spotting," 2024 (arXiv:2404.14903). Cost tensors capture variability across multiple query samples; **using all individual exemplars beats collapsing to Fréchet-mean templates** — the direct algorithmic expression of the root-cause diagnosis.
- **TACos temporally-structured embeddings** — Wilkinghoff & Cornaggia-Urrigshardt, ICASSP 2024 (arXiv:2305.10816). Angular-margin loss producing 2D embeddings that retain temporal structure, used as DTW templates.
- **Multi-enrollment PLDA scoring variants** — Rajan, Afanasyev, Hautamäki, Kinnunen, "From single to multiple enrollment i-vectors," Digital Signal Processing 2014.
- **Articulatory-inspired / segmental representations** — Rudzicz, "Using articulatory likelihoods in the recognition of dysarthric speech," Speech Communication 2012; segmental-DTW on posteriorgrams (Zhang & Glass, ASRU 2009).

**Effect sizes:** LPM is dysarthria-validated with the large recall gains above. On typical low-resource speech, DTW-keyword-spotting (best-of-exemplars) reached AUC 0.75 vs 0.66 (single-template QbyE) vs 0.54 (CNN) (Menon et al. 2018) — showing that retaining multiple exemplars is what buys separability. Multi-sample DTW's central finding — that keeping individual samples beats mean/Fréchet templates — matches the measured cause exactly.

**On-device feasibility:** DTW over stored per-command exemplar templates is deterministic, INT8-friendly, and needs no training at enrollment. Storage grows with #commands × #reps × frames but stays well within ≤150 MB for a constrained command set. **PASS — best-fitting admissibility profile of all five.**

**Assessment — HIGHEST DYSARTHRIA-VALIDATED POTENTIAL.** Keeping the full within-word trajectory distribution (multi-exemplar DTW) instead of a single summary vector directly recovers separability that a mean vector destroys — precisely the measured root cause (intra-speaker within-word variance ≳ between-command distance). Caveat: LPM/KWS metrics are open-set recall/accuracy, not FRR@FAR≤5% in-vocabulary confusor rejection; the confusor task is harder and remains unproven for every candidate.

### P4 — ENROLLMENT-SIDE
**Candidate methods:**
- **Multi-vector retention vs averaging** — Rajan et al. 2014 (averaging best for i-vectors); but multi-sample DTW (P3) shows retention beats averaging for high-variance speech — the two findings diverge exactly because dysarthric speech has CV≈1.
- **Model-quality / outlier-based exemplar selection** — Saeta & Hernando, "Model Quality Evaluation during Enrollment for Speaker Verification"; on-line UBM-based model-quality methods.
- **Number-of-enrollment effect** — multiple sources show monotonic EER improvement K=1→5 (e.g., ECAPA-TDNN/NPLDA: EER 18.4%→10.1% as enrollments grow to K≥5).
- **Severity-scaled reps / active enrollment.**

**Effect sizes:** Enrollment count gives reliable but diminishing EER improvements. On child ASV, in-domain augmentation gives up to 51.52% relative EER reduction (Aziz & Shahnawazuddin, Circuits, Systems & Signal Processing 2025) — but that is augmentation, not selection, and on children.

**On-device feasibility:** Trivial; enrollment-time only. PASS.

**Assessment — LOW-RISK, LOW-CEILING.** Moves the operating point at roughly fixed UX cost but does not change the variance decomposition. Best used as a multiplier on P1/P3, not a standalone D2 fix. Severity-scaled reps (more reps for severe users) is worth testing but collides with the UX reality that severe users find repetition hardest and most fatiguing — the very variability that raises within-word variance also grows with rep count.

### P5 — REFRAME (severe tail)
For very-severe dysarthria (D2≈90%, AUC≈0.70), separability is near information-theoretically capped and no admissible voice-only verifier is deployable. Deterministic, non-LLM system-level rules:
- **Per-severity operating point:** mild (D2≈27%) deploys at FAR≤5% directly; moderate uses a relaxed FAR paired with a confirmation turn; severe abstains by default.
- **Abstain + confirm turn:** a reject-option third outcome (accept / reject / "did you mean X?"), naturally implemented via conformal calibration, trading a confirmation turn for error reduction. This is already standard accessibility-VUI practice (audible/visual confirmation before destructive actions).
- **Complementary modality:** pair voice with switch, dwell, gaze, or head-tracking for the severe tail; multimodal fusion is standard assistive-technology practice and sidesteps the acoustic-separability cap entirely.
- **Achievable bound:** a usable product is reachable for mild/moderate at a fixed FAR; the severe tail requires abstain+confirm or a modality fallback. **Voice-only verification cannot reach a deployable FRR for very-severe dysarthria — this should be treated as a design constraint, not a research target.**

## Recommendations
**Ranked ≤3-experiment shortlist to move AUC 0.70→>0.85 (pre-registered success criteria):**

1. **[HIGHEST] Multi-exemplar trajectory DTW backend (P3), replacing single-vector cosine.** Store all 3–5 enrollment reps per command as WavLM-layer or LPM-style latent trajectories; score by multi-sample DTW (best-of-exemplars, not mean). **Success:** on held-out TORGO/UASpeech, per-severity, FAR-matched at 5%, mild+moderate combined AUC ≥0.85 and D2 ≤30%. **Failure:** AUC <0.78. This bet is most aligned with the measured root cause and has the only dysarthria-validated precedent (LPM). Fastest to run — no offline training.

2. **[HIGH] Trainable constrained/discriminative PLDA or LDA-WCCN backend on frozen WavLM embeddings (P2), trained offline on out-of-domain dysarthric corpora.** **Success:** held-out, per-severity, FAR-matched, ≥8-point AUC gain over cosine on moderate severity, with gains preserved on held-out males (guarding against the refuted whitening artifact). **Failure:** <3-point gain, or gains vanish on held-out males. Explicitly a leakage-controlled, held-out-speaker design. Highest-variance, highest-upside bet — run in parallel.

3. **[MEDIUM] AS-norm + non-monotonic quality-aware calibration with per-command adaptive thresholds (P1), stacked on the winner of #1/#2.** **Success:** ≥3-point AUC or ≥5pp D2 improvement over the un-normalized backend at fixed FAR≤5%. **Failure:** <1pp. Cheap enough to always ship if it clears.

**Staged plan:** Run #1 and #3 first (no offline training). If #1 clears AUC≥0.85 for mild+moderate, ship those severities at fixed FAR and route severe to the P5 abstain+confirm path immediately. Run #2 in parallel. **Threshold that changes the plan:** if none of #1–#3 clears AUC 0.80 on moderate-severity held-out data, freeze verifier R&D for the severe tail and invest entirely in P5 (multimodal + confirm), because the wall is then confirmed information-theoretic for that population.

## Caveats
- **External-validity risk is the dominant caveat.** P1 and P2 effect sizes are almost entirely from typical-speech benchmarks (VoxCeleb, SITW, LibriSpeech), which do not exhibit the CV≈1 within-word intra-speaker variance that is this problem's root cause. Treat all such numbers as upper bounds that may not transfer. Notably, on typical large-margin embeddings cosine already beats conventional PLDA by ~51% relative EER — so the P2 backend gain is entirely contingent on dysarthric embeddings NOT having pre-shrunk within-speaker covariance, which is plausible but unmeasured.
- Only **P3 (LPM)** has direct dysarthric validation, and even there the metric is open-set recall/accuracy, not FRR@FAR≤5% in-vocabulary confusor rejection — the actual D2 task is harder and unproven for every candidate.
- There is **no published isolated PLDA/backend-vs-cosine measurement on dysarthric embeddings** (subagent-confirmed gap); P2 rests on extrapolation from child/style-varied speech, where PLDA adaptation gave real but modest gains (e.g., 22.12%→18.75% EER under style mismatch).
- **TORGO n=8 is very small**; per-severity cells (e.g., "severe" = F01/M01/M02/M04) are dominated by individual speakers, so held-out-speaker generalization claims are fragile. Same-demographic controls do not establish generalization — the refuted female-only whitening result (+10.6pp collapsing to −1.4pp, p=0.63 on held-out males) is the cautionary precedent every experiment above must control for.
- **Simulator-based dysarthric evidence is excluded by requirement**; any candidate validated only on synthetic dysarthria fails the fidelity gate. Require real corpora (TORGO/UASpeech/EasyCall/Nemours), per-severity, held-out speaker, FAR-matched.
- The distilled-student re-examination under the ≤150 MB envelope (vs the old ≤2 MB cap) was not resolved by this search pass; it is orthogonal to the D2 wall (an encoder-capacity question, and the encoder is separability-invariant here), so it is deprioritized relative to P1–P3.
